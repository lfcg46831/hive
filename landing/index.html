01
The problem

AI agents don't fit your org chart.

Bolting chatbots onto a business creates ungoverned, untraceable work. HIVE makes agents first-class members of a real organization — with rank, rules, and a paper trail.

No chain of command

Loose agents act without a superior, peers, or anyone to escalate to. Work happens with no accountability.

No audit trail

You can't reconstruct who decided what, when, or why. Compliance and post-mortems become guesswork.

Runaway cost

Unbounded loops and proactive runs burn tokens. There's no budget, no ceiling, no per-team accounting.

Humans left out

People can't sit in the loop as approvers and teammates — they're an afterthought, not part of the structure.

02
The model

Everything is an actor.

Positions, units, connectors, the AI gateway — all live as supervised actors that talk only through typed messages. The result is an organization you can reason about.

CONCEPT / A

Position ≠ Occupant

The structure is independent of who fills each seat. Swapping an AI for a human is changing the occupant, not the position — inbox and history stay put.

CONCEPT / B

Messages only

No actor touches another's state. People speak to the system through a proxy actor. Every exchange follows a canonical message protocol.

CONCEPT / C

Provider-neutral AI

The AI layer is an abstraction. Anthropic, OpenAI, Azure, or local models via Ollama/vLLM — configured per position, hot-swappable, no actor knows the difference.

CONCEPT / D

Supervision & resilience

Provider timeouts, invalid responses, exceptions — all handled by the Akka.NET supervision hierarchy: restart, escalate, or notify the organizational superior.

CONCEPT / E

Total auditability

Every message is event-sourced. The organization has reconstructable institutional memory, reconciled with GDPR right-to-erasure by design.

CONCEPT / F

Cost is first-class

Every model call has a price. HIVE measures it, caps it with per-position and per-unit budgets, and reports it. Proactive work has its own separate budget.

03
Communication

Three channels, all governed.

HIVE separates how work flows from how it's authorized — so no agent can quietly route around the rules.

↕

Vertical · Command

Directives flow down and get decomposed into sub-directives. Reports and escalations flow up — only ever to your direct superior.

↔

Horizontal · Collaboration

Peers in a unit exchange memos and requests freely. Cross-unit work needs an explicit contract — leaders mediate the rest.

⊕

Governance · Approvals

Approval requests route by authority policy. A decision counts only from the authorized approver, on the right thread. Fail-closed, always.

How it works

From a directive to a result.

One message moves through the organization the way a real task would — delegated, executed, reported, and recorded.

1

Directive arrives

A superior issues a typed directive to a position. The PositionActor persists it to the inbox.

2

Agent builds context

Identity prompt + memory + message + authorized tools go to the provider-neutral AI gateway.

3

It acts

Answer, call a tool, delegate to subordinates, ask a peer, or request human approval — within iteration, budget, and time limits.

4

Report & audit

Memory updates, results report upward, and every step is event-sourced for the institutional record.

09
Questions

FAQ

Is HIVE another agent framework?
No. HIVE is an organizational layer. Agents are members of a real command structure with positions, units, a typed message protocol, governance, and audit — not a chain of prompts. It runs on the Actor Model (Akka.NET) for supervision and resilience.
Which AI providers are supported?
HIVE is provider-neutral via Microsoft.Extensions.AI. Anthropic, OpenAI, Azure OpenAI, and local models (Ollama, vLLM) can coexist, configured per position and hot-swapped without any actor knowing the difference.
How do humans participate?
A person occupies a position through a proxy actor that relays typed messages via connectors — email, Teams, Slack, or the app. The system assumes humans answer in hours or days, so timeouts, reminders, and delegation are designed around that latency.
How do you keep AI costs under control?
Cost is a first-class dimension. Every call is measured, capped by per-position and per-unit budgets, and reported. Proactive activity draws from a separate budget so scheduled routines can never cannibalize the capacity to respond to orders.
Is it auditable and GDPR-compliant?
Yes. All communication is event-sourced into immutable, append-only records that avoid direct PII and point to redactable or encrypted data, reconciling a complete audit trail with the right to erasure under a European GDPR baseline.
How is an organization defined?
As versioned YAML in Git (GitOps) — units, positions, occupants, prompts, schedules, and authority. On deploy it's validated and materialized into PostgreSQL for fast lookups by the API, actors, and UI. The console is read-only first; visual editing arrives in later phases with export back to YAML.
